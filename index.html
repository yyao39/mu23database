SEARCH FOR $\mu_{23}$ VALUES

$\mu_{23}$ is the chemical potential derivative (the subscripts 1, 2, and 3 refer to water, biopolymer/model compound, and solute, respectively). The value of $\mu_{23}$ is a measure of the preferential interaction of a solute with a biopolymer or model compound, where a negative value indicates a favorable preferential interaction and a positive value indicates an unfavorable preferential interaction $\textit{relative to interactions with water}$.1

(1) Guinn, Emily J., et al. "Quantifying functional group interactions that determine urea effects on nucleic acid helix formation." $\textit{Journal of the American Chemical Society}$ 135.15 (2013): 5828-5838.

SEARCH FOR $\alpha$ VALUES

Values of $\mu_{23}$ for protein model compounds were dissected in an ASA-based analysis to determine interactions of solute with individual biopolymer-related functional groups (ASA stands for accessible surface area).1 $$\mu_{23} = \sum_{i} \alpha_i(ASA)_i$$

(1) Guinn, Emily J., et al. "Quantifying functional group interactions that determine urea effects on nucleic acid helix formation." $\textit{Journal of the American Chemical Society}$ 135.15 (2013): 5828-5838.
